# Overview

ASIX will deliver a new class of hybrid, room-temperature X-ray detectors for single-photon,
event-driven, spectral imaging. The detectors will have both high spatial and spectral
resolution and will be suitable for large data throughput applications for space observations and material science. This will be achieved by coupling custom, pixelated,
large area front-end ASICs, equipped with analog readout and smart self-triggering logic,
to finely segmented silicon sensors with similar patterns, both down to a 50 μm hexagonal
pitch. The project will engineer, qualify, deliver and document detector prototypes up to
a Technology Readiness Level (TRL) of 6, and develop the associated readout electronics
and software.

## Heritage

The project builds upon the heritage of our team in the development and operation of
detectors and detector systems for space applications, most notably the Gas Pixel
Detectors for the Imaging X-ray Polarimetry Explorer (IXPE) mission, described in
[Baldini et al. 2021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.astropartphys.2021.102628).


## The ASIX prototype

We have successfully assembled and tested prototypes of the ASIX demonstrator by
leveraging the XPOL-III analog readout ASIC described in
[Minuti et al. 2022](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nima.2022.167674),
coupled to high-efficiency, solid state photon converters through standard bump-bonding
techniques.
<img src="../assets/images/asix_prototype.jpg" width="460"
     style="float: right; margin: 1em;">
These prototypes serve a number of different purposes: validate the basic design
principles and our Monte Carlo simulation, consolidate the sensor requirements,
measure relevant performance metrics (e.g. the effect of the charge sharing and the
scaling of the energy and spatial resolution resolution with the thickness of the
sensor and the pixel noise), and define a set of different configurations tailored
to specific applications&mdash;e.g., optimized for the best possible spectral or
imaging performance. They provide the natural test bed for the development and
optimization of the data-acquisition system and the associated data processing software.
In addition, we are using these first prototypes to inform and guide the implementation
of the detectors verification strategy, and ultimately the definition of the final
design to be engineered and qualified up to TRL 6 (system prototype demonstration in
the relevant environment).

More information about the performance of the prototypes can be found in the
[publication](publications.md) page.
